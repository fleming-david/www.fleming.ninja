About

Who am I?

My name is David Fleming and I am studying computer science at George Mason University in the United States of America. When it comes to computers I have always had a strong passion for them. I loved taking them apart and rebuilding them, trying new things, and seeing what would break and then fixing it. It is through this passion that I have developed a strong understanding of computers and computer systems. My interests in computer science mostly relate to robotics, networking, and systems, though I also have a soft spot for artificial intelligence, machine learning, data mining, and decentralized systems.

Education

Undergraduate at George Mason University studying Computer Science from 2012 to 2017.

A Few Projects

Here are a few projects I have worked on that I feel are my best examples.

Flockbots

The Flockbots are an open source robotics platform developed at George Mason University for the study or robotics and swarm robotics. I have been involved with their development sense V2 (2013), both in hardware and software. Version 3, as of late 2015, is in development with a hardware upgrade and a complete software rewrite. Further information can be found at the wiki and the git repositories are located at gitlab.com/groups/flockbots
